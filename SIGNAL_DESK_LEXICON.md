What This Document Is
This is the terminology registry for Signal Desk — the official record of concepts Wilson L Yang has introduced to the capital-tech-AI-global-meets-China discourse.
Each entry serves three functions simultaneously:

Concept definition — so the term is used precisely, not loosely
Authorship timestamp — establishing first-coinage with verifiable dates and source links
Citation tracking — recording how the term propagates in external discourse

The Lexicon is both an asset ledger and a discipline. Every new term that meets the criteria below gets a permanent entry. Terms that don't pass don't get coined at all.

Lexicon Bar (Six Criteria)
A term is added to the Lexicon only if it passes all six:

Describes a reproducible structure, not a one-time event
Visually or metaphorically resonant — the reader "sees" it on first hearing
Short — 2 to 4 words maximum
Grammatically functions as a noun and can be inserted into sentences naturally
Google-clean — verified via the mandatory three-search protocol (see below)
Extends into a word family — spawns related subterms

Six out of six → coin. Anything less → do not force.

Mandatory Three-Search Verification Protocol
Before any term is added to the Lexicon, Wilson L Yang (or any assistant preparing a recommendation) must execute three independent web searches and record the findings:

Primary context search: "[exact phrase]" + primary domain keywords (e.g., "finance", "capital markets", "valuation")
Adjacent context search: "[exact phrase]" + adjacent domains where the phrase might have independent established use (e.g., "real estate", "tech", "legal", "academic")
Variant search: "[exact phrase]" OR "[plausible variant]" to catch alternative phrasings

Disposition:
FindingActionZero relevant prominent results across all three searches✅ CLEAN — proceed to coinResults exist but in unrelated domain only (e.g., physics, biology)✅ CLEAN with caveat — note in Lexicon entryEstablished use in adjacent professional domain❌ DO NOT COIN — renameEstablished use in same domain❌ DO NOT COIN — this is an existing term, not coinable
Confidence framing: "Clean based on search" ≠ "Never used anywhere, ever." Search engines do not index podcasts, paywalled journals, foreign-language sources exhaustively, or private research notes. The realistic confidence ceiling is ~95% that no prominent prior authorship exists; the residual 5% covers obscure or non-indexed usage. For IP defense purposes this is sufficient, because any challenger must produce public, searchable, timestamped prior art to contest authorship.
Enforcement: Any Lexicon entry lacking recorded three-search verification is provisional and must be verified before external use.

Definition
The Partition Map is a 2×2 framework mapping AI assets across two axes:

Market type: private vs. public
Buyer nationality: global capital vs. China-connected capital

The framework produces four quadrants, each with independent pricing logic and a distinct buyer base. A single underlying asset class — Chinese AI equity — can trade at extreme discounts in one quadrant while trading at extreme premia in another, without contradiction. The same structural event that creates one creates the other.
Core insight: Prices that look irreconcilable are actually internally consistent once you map which quadrant is doing the pricing.
Canonical application: Explaining why DeepSeek is valued at $10B (85:1 discount vs OpenAI) in private markets while MiniMax trades at 425x sales in Hong Kong — simultaneously.
Word family:

The Mandate Discount (the mechanism producing the global-private quadrant's discount on Chinese AI)
Capital Partition (the underlying structural split creating the quadrants)
Cross-Partition Flow (capital movement attempts between quadrants)
Partition Convergence (the hypothetical future collapse of the discount-premium gap)
Partition Arbitrage (strategies attempting to exploit the price differential)

Quadrant labels (used descriptively within the Partition Map, not as independent coinages):

The Global Premium (global-capital × private-market)
The Mandate Discount (global-capital × constrained access to Chinese private AI)
The Liquid Benchmark (global-capital × public-market)
The Scarcity Bubble (China-connected capital × public-market, e.g. Hong Kong AI listings)

Usage pattern:
First mention in any piece: "the Signal Desk Partition Map" (brand-bound)
Subsequent: "the Partition Map" or "this partition"
Example sentences:

"Under the Signal Desk Partition Map, MiniMax's 425x multiple is not a bubble — it's a quadrant artifact."
"The Pony.ai listing is a test case for Partition Convergence."

Definition
The Mandate Discount is the structural valuation discount applied to a class of assets because the dominant capital pool pricing that asset class operates under mandate constraints — LP agreements, fiduciary rules, regulatory restrictions — that limit or exclude holding the assets, independent of their technical or commercial quality.
It is distinct from:

Risk premium (compensation for risk actually borne by the holder)
Illiquidity discount (compensation for inability to exit)
Discount for lack of marketability (private-market valuation adjustment)

The Mandate Discount is specifically partition-derived: it is produced by the structural sorting of capital into pools that can and cannot hold certain asset classes, not by the assets themselves.
Diagnostic test: If the same asset would trade at a materially different price in a different capital pool with different mandate constraints — and the capability/commercial profile is substantially similar — the differential is a Mandate Discount, not a risk premium.
Canonical data anchor: DeepSeek at $10B vs OpenAI at $852B → 85:1 ratio (April 2026), despite Stanford AI Index showing only a 2.7-point capability gap. The 85:1 ratio cannot be explained by the technology gap; it is explained by mandate-driven withdrawal of global dollar capital from the asset class (13F filings of Appaloosa, Bridgewater, Scion across 2025).
Word family:

Mandate Constraint (the LP / fiduciary provision producing the discount)
Mandate Asymmetry (when two pools face different mandate constraints on the same asset)
Mandate Rotation (systematic withdrawal of a capital pool due to mandate changes)

Relationship to Partition Map: The Mandate Discount is the mechanism producing the price differential between Partition Map quadrants. Without mandate constraints on global capital, the China Discount quadrant would not exist at current magnitude — assets would re-price to global comparables.
Relationship to "The China Discount": "The China Discount" is a pre-existing industry term (e.g., used in David Lau 2023, Rothschild & Co 2024, Federated Hermes 2023) that describes the phenomenon of Chinese equity valuation discounts relative to US peers. The Mandate Discount is the underlying mechanism that produces the China Discount (and analogous discounts in other asset classes where capital partition applies). Signal Desk uses "China Discount" descriptively when referring to the surface phenomenon, and "Mandate Discount" when referring to the structural mechanism.
Usage pattern:
First mention: "the Signal Desk Mandate Discount" or "what I'll call the Mandate Discount"
Subsequent: "the Mandate Discount"
Example sentences:

"The 85:1 ratio on DeepSeek is a Mandate Discount, not a risk premium — LP constraints on US-domiciled capital cannot be arbitraged away by conviction alone."
"Watch for Mandate Rotation in Q2 as new EU pension fiduciary rules take effect."
"The Mandate Discount generalizes beyond AI: the same mechanism prices Chinese pharma, semis, and defense-adjacent assets across capital pools."

The Capability-Price Decoupling
Category: Capital × Tech × Global-meets-China
Status: Cross-cutting framework; applies beyond AI
Coined: 2026-04-20

Definition
A structural pattern where the technical capability gap between two groups of firms is converging toward zero, while the market price differential between them is diverging — moving in opposite directions simultaneously.
The Capability-Price Decoupling is the underlying anomaly that makes the Partition Map necessary. If capability and price moved together, partition-based pricing would not produce persistent mispricing. They don't — which is why the Partition exists as a pricing regime rather than a transient dislocation.
Canonical application: In April 2026, Chinese frontier AI labs sit 2.7 points behind US labs on Stanford AI Index capability benchmarks (a gap that has shrunk roughly 5x over 24 months), while trading at valuation discounts up to 85:1 (a gap that has widened materially over the same period).
Diagnostic question: "If capability is converging, what is price responding to?" — The answer is never "capability," and is usually a Mandate Constraint.
Word family:

Capability Convergence (the numerator collapsing toward zero)
Price Divergence (the denominator expanding)
The Decoupling Anomaly (the paired motion itself)

Usage pattern:
First mention: "the Signal Desk Capability-Price Decoupling"
Subsequent: "the Capability-Price Decoupling" or "the Decoupling"
Example sentences:

"The Capability-Price Decoupling is not unique to AI — the same pattern appears in EV and robotics."
"If you see Capability Convergence without Price Convergence, a Mandate Constraint is operating."

Terms Explicitly Not Coined by Signal Desk (for the avoidance of doubt)
This section exists to prevent inadvertent authorship overclaims.
"The China Discount" — pre-existing industry term widely used before Signal Desk's establishment. Signal Desk uses this phrase descriptively when referring to the surface-level phenomenon of Chinese equity valuation discounts. The underlying mechanism Signal Desk claims authorship over is The Mandate Discount (see entry above). Prior uses observed: David Lau (Medium, 2023), Rothschild & Co (2024), Federated Hermes (2023), Invesco (2026), Columbia Business School working papers, and others.
"The Global Premium", "The Liquid Benchmark", "The Scarcity Bubble" — used as descriptive quadrant labels within the Partition Map. These are not independently coined Signal Desk terms. "Scarcity premium" is widely used in finance; "Scarcity Stack" was coined by another author in early 2026.
"Scarcity premium" — standard finance term, widely used. Not a Signal Desk term.
"Decoupling" — standard finance term (US/EM decoupling, etc.). Signal Desk's contribution is the specific compound Capability-Price Decoupling, not "decoupling" itself.
