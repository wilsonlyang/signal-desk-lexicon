## What This Document Is

This is the terminology registry for Signal Desk — the official record of concepts Wilson L Yang has introduced to the capital-tech-AI-global-meets-China discourse.

Each entry serves three functions simultaneously:

1. **Concept definition** — so the term is used precisely, not loosely
2. **Authorship timestamp** — establishing first-coinage with verifiable dates and source links
3. **Citation tracking** — recording how the term propagates in external discourse

The Lexicon is both an asset ledger and a discipline. Every new term that meets the six-criteria bar (see § Lexicon Bar) gets a permanent entry. Terms that don't meet the bar don't get coined at all.

---

## Lexicon Bar (Six Criteria)

A term is added to the Lexicon only if it passes all six:

1. **Describes a reproducible structure**, not a one-time event
2. **Visually or metaphorically resonant** — the reader "sees" it on first hearing
3. **Short** — 2 to 4 words maximum
4. **Grammatically functions as a noun** and can be inserted into sentences naturally
5. **Google-clean** — no prior prominent occupation of the exact phrase
6. **Extends into a word family** — spawns related subterms

Six out of six → coin. Anything less → do not force. **Discipline beats volume.**

## Lexicon Check (Pre-Publish Routine)

Before publishing any Signal or long-form piece, apply this 5-minute routine:

1. Does this piece describe a reproducible structure?
2. If yes, does it have a 2–4 word name?
3. Does the name pass the six criteria?

**All three yes** → name it explicitly in the piece ("I'll call this the [Name]"), bold it, add entry to Lexicon.

**Any no** → do not force a new term. Proceed to the second check:

4. Does any existing Lexicon entry apply to this piece?
5. If yes, explicitly reference it ("Under the Signal Desk Partition Map, ...") — this reinforces existing concepts through repeated citation.

### Partition Map

**Also known as**: The Signal Desk Partition Map, The AI Capital Partition
**Category**: Capital × Global-meets-China
**Status**: Flagship concept

**Coined**: 2026-04-20
**Coined in**: *The China AI Discount and the Hong Kong Premium Are the Same Trade*

**OpenTimestamps proof**: [to be filled if using]
**GitHub commit**: [to be filled after first commit]

**Definition**

The Partition Map is a 2×2 framework mapping AI assets across two axes:

- **Market type**: private vs. public
- **Buyer nationality**: global capital vs. China-connected capital

The framework produces four quadrants, each with independent pricing logic and a distinct buyer base. A single underlying asset class — Chinese AI equity — can trade at extreme discounts in one quadrant (private markets × global capital) while trading at extreme premia in another (public markets × China-connected capital), without contradiction. The same event that creates one creates the other.

**Core insight**: Prices that look irreconcilable are actually internally consistent once you map which quadrant is doing the pricing.

**Canonical application**: Explaining why DeepSeek trades at $10B (85:1 discount vs OpenAI) in private markets while MiniMax trades at 425x sales in Hong Kong — simultaneously.

**Word family**:
- **The China Discount** (global-private quadrant pricing of Chinese AI assets)
- **The Scarcity Bubble** (China-public quadrant pricing of Chinese AI assets)
- **Capital Partition** (the underlying structural split creating the quadrants)
- **Cross-Partition Flow** (capital movement attempts between quadrants)
- **Partition Convergence** (the hypothetical future collapse of the discount-premium gap)
- **Partition Arbitrage** (strategies attempting to exploit the price differential)

**Usage pattern**:

First mention in any piece: "the Signal Desk Partition Map" (brand-bound)
Subsequent: "the Partition Map" or "this partition"

Example sentences:
- "Under the Signal Desk Partition Map, MiniMax's 425x multiple is not a bubble — it's a quadrant artifact."
- "The Pony.ai listing is a test case for Partition Convergence."

### The China Discount

**Category**: Capital × Global-meets-China
**Status**: Subterm under Partition Map; independently citable

**Coined**: 2026-04-20
**Coined in**: *The China AI Discount and the Hong Kong Premium Are the Same Trade*

**Definition**

The structural valuation discount applied to Chinese technology assets (particularly AI) in private markets dominated by global capital. Distinct from "China risk premium" in public equity literature — the China Discount is specifically a **partition-derived** discount, produced when a capital pool's mandate excludes or restricts exposure to China-connected assets.

**Diagnostic test**: If the same asset would trade at a dramatically different price in a different capital pool with different mandate constraints, the differential is China Discount, not risk premium.

**Canonical data anchor**: DeepSeek at $10B vs OpenAI at $852B → 85:1 ratio (April 2026), despite Stanford AI Index showing only 2.7-point capability gap.

**Word family**:
- **The Scarcity Bubble** (its inverse in the China-public quadrant)
- **Capability-Price Decoupling** (the underlying anomaly the Discount produces)

**Usage pattern**:

First mention: "the Signal Desk China Discount" or "what I'll call the China Discount"
Subsequent: "the China Discount"

Example sentences:
- "The China Discount on DeepSeek is now 85:1 — and the term sheet confirms it."
- "Any framework for Chinese AI valuation must account for the China Discount as a structural feature, not a temporary dislocation."

### The Capability–Price Decoupling

**Category**: Capital × Tech × Global-meets-China
**Status**: Cross-cutting framework; applies beyond AI

**Coined**: 2026-04-20
**Coined in**: *The China AI Discount and the Hong Kong Premium Are the Same Trade* (foundational logic)

**Definition**

A structural pattern where the **technical capability gap** between two groups of firms is converging toward zero, while the **market price differential** between them is diverging — moving in opposite directions simultaneously.

The Capability–Price Decoupling is the underlying anomaly that makes the Partition Map necessary. If capability and price moved together, partition-based pricing would not produce persistent mispricing. They don't — which is why the Partition exists as a pricing regime rather than a transient dislocation.

**Canonical application**: In April 2026, Chinese frontier AI labs sit 2.7 points behind US labs on Stanford AI Index capability benchmarks (a gap that has shrunk roughly 5x over 24 months), while trading at valuation discounts up to 85:1 (a gap that has widened materially over the same period).

**Diagnostic question**: "If capability is converging, what is price responding to?" — The answer is never "capability," and is usually a partition-derived constraint.

**Word family**:
- **Capability Convergence** (the numerator collapsing toward zero)
- **Price Divergence** (the denominator expanding)
- **The Decoupling Anomaly** (the paired motion itself)

**Usage pattern**:

First mention: "the Signal Desk Capability–Price Decoupling"
Subsequent: "the Capability–Price Decoupling" or "the Decoupling"

Example sentences:
- "The Capability–Price Decoupling is not unique to AI — the same pattern appears in EV and robotics."
- "If you see Capability Convergence without Price Convergence, a partition is operating."
