## The Compound Principle

The single most important rule:

**Never let `Wilson L Yang` or `Signal Desk` appear in isolation in public-facing contexts.**

Always bind them together. This creates a compound string — `Wilson L Yang · Signal Desk` — that is globally unique and unambiguously identifies one author at one publication. Google's knowledge graph will, over 6–12 months, recognize them as a single entity; searching either will surface the other.

Every byline, bio, signature, header, footer, and social profile should reinforce this binding.

## The Three-Piece Signature (Absolute Rule)

On every piece of published content — Signal, long-form, chart, video, recording — the following three elements must appear together:

1. **`Wilson L Yang`** (the person)
2. **`Signal Desk`** (the publication)
3. **`signaldeskwilson.substack.com`** (the permanent address)

Position may vary by format (byline, footer, signature block), but **all three must be present on every artifact**. If a screenshot of any single piece of your work is shared in isolation, these three elements must travel with it.

This is the single most important branding rule. It is not optional.
